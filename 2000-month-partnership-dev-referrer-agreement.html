What is a Dev-Referrer Agreement?

A Dev-Referrer Agreement (or Referral Partnership Agreement) is a mutual understanding or formal document that outlines the terms of collaboration between an individual (the referrer) and a development agency (like Offline Pixel Computers). It clarifies expectations for both parties, ensuring a smooth process from lead introduction to commission payout.

Why a Clear Agreement is Crucial for Your $2000/Month Goal:

  • Eliminates Ambiguity: No guesswork on commission rates, payment terms, or lead qualification. Everyone is on the same page.
  • Builds Trust: Transparency fosters confidence, allowing you to refer with conviction and build long-term relationships.
  • Protects Both Parties: Clearly defines responsibilities and ensures fair compensation for your efforts.
  • Streamlines Process: A defined process for lead submission, tracking, and payout makes the entire referral funnel more efficient.
  • Scalability: With clear rules, you can confidently scale your referral efforts, knowing the framework is solid.

Key Elements of a Strong Dev-Referrer Agreement:

1. Commission Structure:

This is the most critical element for you.

  • Percentage/Flat Fee: Clearly states the commission rate (e.g., our 10% commission on project value).
  • Basis of Calculation: Is it on gross project value, net profit, or a specific milestone? (Offline Pixel Computers bases it on the total project value).
  • Applicability: Does it apply to initial projects only, or also to future projects/phases from the same client? (With Offline Pixel Computers, new projects from referred clients generate new commissions).

2. Payment Terms:

When and how will you get paid?

  • Trigger Event: When is the commission earned? (e.g., upon client signing contract, upon first payment, upon project completion and final payment). Offline Pixel Computers pays upon project completion and final payment.
  • Payment Schedule: How soon after the trigger event will payment be made? (e.g., within 7, 15, or 30 days).
  • Payment Method: How will funds be transferred? (e.g., bank transfer, PayPal).

3. Lead Qualification & Submission Process:

How should you submit leads, and what constitutes a "qualified" lead?

  • Definition of Qualified Lead: What criteria must a lead meet (e.g., clear need, budget, decision-maker access, no prior contact with the development agency).
  • Submission Method: How should leads be introduced (e.g., 3-way email, dedicated form).
  • Lead Registration/Tracking: How will the lead be registered to you to ensure proper attribution? (Offline Pixel Computers ensures clear tracking).

4. Communication Protocol:

How will information flow between you, the client, and the development team?

  • Updates: How often will you receive updates on your referred leads' status?
  • Client Contact: Who is the primary point of contact for the client? (Offline Pixel Computers handles all client communication post-introduction).
  • Feedback: A mechanism for you to provide feedback and for us to provide insights on lead quality.

5. Term & Termination:

The duration of the agreement and conditions for ending the partnership.

  • Duration: Is it ongoing, or for a fixed term?
  • Termination Clauses: Conditions under which either party can terminate the agreement.
