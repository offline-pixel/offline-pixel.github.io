Simple Places to Find Web Dev Referrals (USA-Wide)

You don't need to be a marketing guru. Opportunities are all around you:

1. Your Immediate Surroundings & Local Businesses

Start with what you know and see every day:

  • Local Shops & Services: The diner down the street, your barber, the local florist, a neighborhood accountant, or even your dentist. Many small businesses have outdated or missing online presences.
  • Community Groups & Events: Look at local clubs, non-profits, or upcoming community events. Often, these organizations could use a better website.
  • Friends & Family: Simply let everyone in your personal network know that you can connect businesses to professional web developers. Word-of-mouth is powerful.

2. Quick Online Checks (2-Minute Opportunities)

A few clicks can reveal many leads:

  • Google Maps Scan: Open Google Maps, search for "restaurants near me" or "plumbers [your city]". Click on businesses that don't have a website listed or whose website looks very old.
  • Social Media Bios: Check the Instagram or Facebook profiles of local businesses. If their link in bio goes to a generic page, or a very basic site, they're a potential lead.
  • Online Reviews: Sometimes, businesses get negative reviews related to their website or online ordering issues. This is a clear signal they need help.

3. Niche-Specific & Growth Areas

For slightly more targeted searches, consider areas where new businesses frequently emerge:

  • **New Business Listings:** Check local county or state public records for newly registered businesses. They'll need a website from day one.
  • **Small Consulting Firms or Freelancers:** They often need professional sites to attract their own clients.
  • **Any Business with a Product/Service:** If someone is selling *anything*, they can benefit from an effective online presence.
