The Pathway to $1750/Month: A Staged Approach

Stage 1: The First Breakthrough (Target: $250 - $500/month)

This is where the excitement begins. Your primary focus here is simply to prove the model to yourself.

  • Strategy: Focus on "low-hanging fruit" – warm introductions within your immediate network (friends, family, past colleagues, local businesses you frequent). Listen for obvious digital pain points.
  • Effort: Minimal. It's about being observant and making a genuine offer to connect.
  • What it looks like: You identify a friend's small business needing a modern website refresh ($2,500 project = $250 commission) or a local service provider needing a basic e-commerce setup ($5,000 project = $500 commission).
  • Key Learning: You see that it works! This initial success builds confidence and demonstrates the value proposition.

Stage 2: Building Momentum (Target: $500 - $1000/month)

Once you've landed your first referral, it's time to intentionally replicate that success and build consistency.

  • Strategy: Systematize your warm introductions. Dedicate specific, consistent time each week to reviewing your network, reaching out to contacts, and listening for needs. Consider reactivating old professional connections on LinkedIn.
  • Effort: Moderate. It requires discipline but still leverages existing trust.
  • What it looks like: You might secure two $5,000 website projects ($1,000 commission) or one larger $10,000 custom web application ($1,000 commission).
  • Key Learning: You start to understand what types of businesses or individuals in your network are most likely to have digital needs. You refine your brief introductory pitch.

Stage 3: Strategic Growth (Target: $1000 - $1750/month)

To cross the $1000 threshold and aim for $1750, you need to become more strategic in your lead generation and qualification.

  • Strategy: Begin to actively seek out businesses with higher-value needs. This might involve targeting specific industries (e.g., e-commerce, real estate, professional services) or connecting with complementary businesses that serve these clients (e.g., marketing agencies, business consultants, IT service providers).
  • Effort: More proactive. You're building new connections with a strategic intent.
  • What it looks like: You might refer one $17,500 custom software project ($1,750 commission) or a combination, such as a $10,000 e-commerce site and a $7,500 web application enhancement ($1,750 commission total).
  • Key Learning: You learn to qualify leads more effectively, identifying pain points that require larger digital solutions. You understand the power of strategic partnerships.

Stage 4: Consistency & Scaling ($1750+/month)

At this stage, your referral income becomes reliable, and you're well-positioned for even further growth.

  • Strategy: Your network has grown, and your reputation as a reliable connector is established. You'll likely receive inbound referrals from past satisfied clients or strategic partners. Continue nurturing these relationships and stay alert for new opportunities.
  • Effort: Sustained and refined. The initial push has paid off, and now it's about maintenance and intelligent expansion.
  • What it looks like: A steady flow of projects ranging from $10,000 to $20,000+, consistently bringing in your $1750+ commission each month.
