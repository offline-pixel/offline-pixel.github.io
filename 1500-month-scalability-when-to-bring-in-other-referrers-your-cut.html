The "Your Cut" Model: How It Works

The concept is simple: you act as a "master referrer" or "referral team lead."

  1. You Introduce Sub-Referrers: You identify individuals who have strong networks and might be interested in earning commissions themselves (e.g., consultants, marketers, accountants, community leaders, even friends with wide professional circles).
  2. They Refer Projects to Offline Pixel Computers: These sub-referrers then introduce their leads directly to Offline Pixel Computers, just as you would.
  3. They Earn Their Commission: Offline Pixel Computers pays your sub-referrers their agreed-upon commission (e.g., 8% of the project value).
  4. You Earn Your Cut: Because you brought them into the program, you receive a smaller percentage (e.g., 1-2%) of the project value for every project they successfully refer. This is your "cut" for building the sub-referral relationship.

This model is designed to incentivize both the sub-referrer (who gets a direct commission) and you (who gets a percentage for building the network). It's a win-win-win: clients get great service, sub-referrers earn, and you scale your income.

The Math for Your $1500/Month Goal:

Let's say you aim for $1500/month.

  • Direct Referrals: You might still aim to bring in one $10,000 project yourself (yielding $1,000 commission).
  • Sub-Referrers' Contribution: If you have 3 sub-referrers, and each refers one $10,000 project, that's $30,000 in total project value. If your cut is 2%, that's an additional $600 for you ($30,000 * 0.02).
  • Total: $1,000 (your direct) + $600 (from sub-referrers) = $1,600/month.

This demonstrates how a small percentage from a larger volume of projects can significantly boost your income without a linear increase in your direct effort.
