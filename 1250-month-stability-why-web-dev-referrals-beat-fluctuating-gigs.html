The Problem with Fluctuating Gig Economy Jobs:

Many popular gig economy roles, while offering flexibility, come with inherent instability:

  • Hourly/Task-Based Pay: Income is directly tied to the hours you work or the number of tasks you complete. If demand is low, or you can't work, your income drops.
  • High Competition: Low barriers to entry mean more competition, driving down rates and availability.
  • No Residual Income: Once a task is done, the income stops. There's no ongoing benefit from past efforts.
  • Burnout: The constant need to chase the next gig can lead to exhaustion and demotivation.
  • Low Ticket Value: Many gigs pay small amounts per task, requiring massive volume to earn significant income.
