Where Your Time Goes: The Breakdown for $2500/Month

To consistently earn $2500 per month, you'll need to secure projects that collectively total around $25,000 in value each month (based on our 10% commission structure). This could be one large $25,000 project, two $12,500 projects, or a few smaller ones that add up. The time commitment isn't about working a fixed number of hours, but about the *strategic allocation* of your effort.

1. Initial Setup & Learning (Front-Loaded Effort):

  • Understanding the Service: Familiarize yourself with Offline Pixel Computers' capabilities – what we build, our process, and our value proposition. (Approx. 2-4 hours initially).
  • Defining Your Niche/Target: Who are you best positioned to reach? What types of businesses or industries do you understand best? (Approx. 1-2 hours).
  • Crafting Your Pitch: Develop a simple, compelling way to introduce the opportunity to potential clients. (Approx. 1-2 hours).

This initial investment of time pays dividends by making subsequent efforts more efficient.

2. Active Lead Generation (Ongoing Strategic Effort):

This is where the majority of your time commitment will lie. It's not about being "busy" but being "effective."

  • Networking: Actively engaging with your existing network (friends, family, past colleagues, professional contacts). This could involve casual conversations, brief emails, or messages. (Approx. 2-4 hours/week).
  • Research & Observation: Identifying potential clients online (LinkedIn, local business directories, industry forums, social media) or offline (local businesses, business events). Looking for outdated websites, manual processes, or expressed needs. (Approx. 3-5 hours/week).
  • Outreach & Initial Contact: Sending personalized emails, LinkedIn messages, or making phone calls to potential leads. This is about sparking interest, not closing the deal. (Approx. 4-6 hours/week).
  • Follow-up & Nurturing: Keeping track of promising leads and following up periodically to maintain interest. (Approx. 2-3 hours/week).

Total Active Lead Gen: Approx. 11-18 hours per week.

3. Lead Qualification (Crucial, but Brief):

  • Engaging in short conversations to determine if a lead has a genuine need, budget, decision-making authority, and timeline (as discussed in previous posts). This saves time for everyone. (Approx. 1-2 hours/week, depending on lead volume).

4. Making Introductions (The Payoff Moment):

  • Once a lead is qualified, facilitating the introduction to Offline Pixel Computers. This is usually a quick email or a brief three-way call setup. (Minimal time, perhaps 15-30 minutes per qualified lead).

The "Reality Check" Time Commitment for $2500/Month:

To consistently hit $2500/month, a realistic time commitment would be in the range of **15-25 strategic hours per week**. This is not "working" in the traditional sense, but rather a focused effort on networking, research, and communication. It's often flexible and can be woven into your existing schedule.

This contrasts sharply with many side gigs where 15-25 hours might net you a few hundred dollars. Here, you're investing time in high-value activities that leverage your ability to connect, rather than simply trading hours for a low hourly rate.
