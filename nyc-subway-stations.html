Top 50 Busiest NYC Subway Stations (2024 Ridership Data)

offline-pixel · 6 min read · Today

New York City's subway system is the lifeblood of the city, carrying millions of commuters daily. But which stations handle the most traffic? Based on recent MTA data, we've ranked the top 50 busiest subway stations by average weekday ridership.

Key Findings

  • Times Square-42 St remains the busiest station, serving over 450,000 daily riders across 12 subway lines.
  • Manhattan dominates the top rankings, with Grand Central-42 St and 34 St-Herald Sq rounding out the top 3.
  • The busiest Brooklyn station is Atlantic Av-Barclays Center, ranking #9 overall.
  • Queens' busiest station is 74 St-Broadway in Jackson Heights at #11.
  • Even the 50th busiest station (Dyckman St) sees about 5,000 daily riders.

Analysis

The data reveals several interesting patterns about NYC's subway usage:

  • Transfer Stations Dominate: The top stations are major transfer points (Times Square, Herald Sq, Union Sq) where multiple lines intersect.
  • Commuter Hubs: Stations near major employment centers (Grand Central, Penn Station, WTC) see heavy inbound morning traffic.
  • Tourist Impact: Times Square's #1 ranking reflects both commuter and tourist traffic.
  • Borough Distribution: Only 4 of the top 50 stations are outside Manhattan (9 in Brooklyn, 1 in Queens, 1 in Bronx).

Note: Ridership numbers are approximate averages based on recent MTA data and may vary seasonally.
