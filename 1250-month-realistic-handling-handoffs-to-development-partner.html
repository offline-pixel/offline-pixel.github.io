Key Elements of a Successful Client Handoff:

Your goal is to make the introduction as easy and informative as possible for both your contact and Offline Pixel Computers. Think of yourself as a bridge, ensuring everyone is on the same page.

1. The Warm Introduction (Personal & Direct)

  • The Preferred Method: A 3-Way Email: This is often the most effective. Send an email introducing your client to our team (e.g., to your dedicated contact or our general inquiries email).
  • Key Components of the Email:
    • Briefly introduce your client: Their name, company, and what they do.
    • Briefly introduce Offline Pixel Computers: As your trusted web/software development partner, highlighting our expertise.
    • State the client's core need: "John's business, [Client Co.], is looking to [their specific goal, e.g., build a new e-commerce platform / develop a custom inventory management system / redesign their outdated website]."
    • Suggest the Next Step: "I thought it would be great for you two to connect for a quick chat to discuss their needs in more detail."
    • Set the Stage for the Client: Explicitly tell the client that Offline Pixel Computers will be reaching out to schedule a discovery call.
  • Consider a 3-Way Call: If the client is very engaged and comfortable, a brief 10-15 minute video or phone call where you introduce both parties directly can be highly effective.

2. Provide Initial Context (Briefing Your Partner)

Even better than the email, provide our team with some pre-call notes. This can be in the body of the introduction email or a separate quick message. This helps us prepare effectively and make a strong first impression.

  • What to Include:
    • Client's Core Pain Point/Goal: What specific problem are they trying to solve?
    • Any Known Budget/Timeline (even rough): "They mentioned a budget around X" or "They're hoping to launch by Q4."
    • Key Decision-Makers: Who else might be involved in the decision?
    • Any Specific Technologies Mentioned: "They use Shopify" or "They need Salesforce integration."
    • How you know them: Provides context for our team.

3. Ensure Client Readiness & Set Expectations:

Before you make the introduction, ensure your client understands what's happening. They should be expecting a call or email from our team and be ready to discuss their project needs.

  • Pre-Handoff Chat: "I'm going to connect you with my development partners at Offline Pixel Computers. They're great at taking ideas and turning them into reality. They'll reach out to set up a short call to understand your needs and see how they can help. Does that sound good?"

4. Your Continued (Limited) Involvement:

After the handoff, your main role is complete. However, you can offer to be a point of contact if either party has a quick question, or simply check in with both sides to ensure the conversation has happened smoothly. This keeps you in the loop without burdening you with project management.
