Finding Your Passive(ish) Referral Opportunities in the USA

The demand for web development is ubiquitous across the U.S. Here are evergreen sources for potential referral fees:

1. Small & Medium Businesses (SMBs) Across All Sectors

These are the backbone of the economy, and many are still catching up digitally.

  • Local Service Businesses: Salons, barbershops, car repair, cleaners, landscapers, independent contractors – many need modern websites for bookings, service listings, and credibility.
  • Healthcare & Wellness Professionals: Private practices (dentists, therapists, chiropractors), yoga studios, fitness coaches – they often need appointment scheduling, client portals, and informational sites.
  • Restaurants & Cafes: Beyond basic menus, many need online ordering systems, reservation features, and improved mobile experiences.

2. Networks You Already Have

The easiest referrals often come from within your existing circles.

  • Personal & Professional Connections: Friends, family, former colleagues, acquaintances. Let everyone know you're connected to a great web development team.
  • Community Groups: Local clubs, volunteer organizations, church groups, school boards. People within these networks often have businesses or know someone who does.
  • Online Forums & Groups: Join local business groups on Facebook or LinkedIn. Pay attention to questions about web designers or struggles with online presence.

3. Observational & Digital Scrutiny

A little online detective work can uncover significant opportunities.

  • "No Website" Businesses: Use Google Maps to browse local businesses. Look for those without a website link or with only a social media presence.
  • Outdated Websites: Visit websites of local businesses. If they look old, are not mobile-friendly, or are difficult to navigate, they are prime candidates for a redesign.
  • New Business Filings: In many states/counties, new business registrations are public. These new ventures often need a website from day one.
