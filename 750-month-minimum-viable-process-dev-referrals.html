The Minimum Viable Process for $750/Month in Dev Referrals:

This streamlined approach focuses on observation, light qualification, and effective connection. It's about being a helpful bridge, not a burdensome salesperson.

Step 1: Identify a Potential Need (The "Listening" Phase)

This is the most low-effort part. You don't need to actively "sell" or cold call. Instead, simply be observant and listen in your everyday interactions:

  • Casual Conversations: Are friends, family, or acquaintances who own businesses complaining about their website, outdated systems, or lack of online functionality?
  • Observation: Do you see local businesses with clearly outdated, non-mobile-friendly, or non-existent websites? Do they rely heavily on manual processes that could be automated?
  • Social Media: Are people in your network asking for recommendations for web designers or developers, or expressing frustration with their current digital tools?

Focus: Look for clear signs that a business needs a new website, an upgrade, an e-commerce store, or a custom internal tool.

Step 2: Qualify (Lightly & Briefly)

Once you identify a potential need, a very light qualification helps ensure you're connecting a truly viable lead. This isn't a deep dive, just a quick check for seriousness.

  • One Simple Question: "Have you considered getting that problem fixed?" or "Are you looking to make a change to your website/system anytime soon?"
  • Listen for Intent: Are they just complaining, or do they express a genuine desire to find a solution? Do they mention any timeframe or budget (even vague)?

Focus: Filter out casual complaints from actual potential projects. Your goal is a "warm" lead.

Step 3: Make the Warm Introduction (The Critical Connection)

This is the most crucial step. A direct, personal introduction is key to success.

  • Method: A 3-way email introduction is often the simplest and most effective. Alternatively, a brief 3-way phone/video call if both parties are amenable.
  • What to Include:
    • Briefly introduce your contact and their business.
    • Briefly introduce Offline Pixel Computers as your trusted development partner.
    • State the core problem/need that prompted the introduction (e.g., "John is looking to upgrade his outdated e-commerce store," or "Sarah's company needs a custom tool for inventory management").
    • Suggest the next step: "I thought it would be great for you two to connect for a quick chat to discuss this further."

Focus: Create a smooth, trust-based handoff from you to our expert team.

Step 4: Step Back (Let Our Team Handle the Hard Part)

This is where the "low effort" truly comes into play. Once the introduction is made, your active role diminishes significantly.

  • Trust Our Process: Offline Pixel Computers will take over. We handle the detailed discovery calls, proposals, sales closing, project management, and development.
  • Avoid Involvement: Do not get involved in the sales calls, technical discussions, or project updates unless specifically asked by *both* parties (which is rare).

Focus: Allow our team to do what they do best, freeing up your time.

Step 5: Track (Simply for Yourself)

A basic spreadsheet or even a note on your phone is all you need to track your referrals.

  • Record: Client Name, Company, Date of Intro, Potential Project Type.
  • Purpose: This helps you keep tabs on your potential earnings and identify patterns in successful referrals. Our team will keep you informed on the progress of your referred leads.

Focus: Maintain a clear overview of your referral pipeline without creating extra work.
