Your Arrival at Delhi Airport (IGI): First Steps for Foreign Travellers

Stepping off the plane at Indira Gandhi International Airport (IGI) in Delhi is the beginning of your North India journey. Here's what foreign travellers need to expect and how to navigate your first hours:

Pro Tip for Delhi Airport: Keep copies of your e-Visa printout, passport, and flight itinerary easily accessible. Have a pen ready for any forms to ensure a smooth immigration process.
  • Immigration & Visa: Have your passport and valid Indian visa (e-Visa printout or stamped visa) ready. Be prepared for queues, especially during peak seasons.
  • Currency Exchange: You'll find official currency exchange counters and ATMs in the arrival hall. Exchange a small amount for immediate needs.
  • Local SIM Card for Travellers: Consider purchasing a local SIM card from providers like Airtel, Jio, or Vodafone Idea at the airport kiosks. You'll need your passport, visa, and a passport-sized photo. This is essential for staying connected as a solo traveller in Delhi.
  • Baggage Claim: Follow signs to retrieve your luggage. Double-check your baggage tags.

Safe & Easy Transport from New Delhi Airport for All Travellers

Your first ride from New Delhi Airport is crucial. Avoid potential tourist scams by using trusted options, especially important for solo and female travellers:

Recommended Transport Options in Delhi

Airport Express Metro Pre-paid Taxis Uber / Ola (Ride-hailing Apps) Hotel Transfers
  • Airport Express Metro (Orange Line): The fastest and most affordable way to central Delhi (New Delhi Railway Station, Shivaji Stadium). Clean, efficient, and avoids traffic. It's often recommended for solo female travellers in Delhi for its safety.
  • Official Pre-paid Taxis: Located just outside the terminal. Pay at the counter, get a receipt, and you'll be assigned a taxi. Ensures a fixed fare and helps prevent overcharging.
  • Ride-Hailing Apps (Uber/Ola): Download these apps before arrival. You can book a cab from designated pick-up points at the airport. Convenient and offers transparent pricing. They often have safety features like live tracking, beneficial for solo travellers in New Delhi.
  • Hotel Transfers: Many hotels offer pre-arranged airport pickups. This is often the most hassle-free option, though usually more expensive, providing peace of mind for foreign travellers in Delhi.

Avoiding Common Tourist Scams in Delhi

Unsolicited Help Fake Tourist Offices "Hotel is Closed" Taxis
  • Ignore Unsolicited Offers: At the airport, decline offers from individuals claiming to be guides, taxi drivers, or helpful strangers. Stick to official counters. This is a common tactic, particularly for foreign travellers in New Delhi.
  • Beware of Fake "Tourist Information" Booths: Only use government-approved tourist information centers or your hotel for advice.
  • "Your Hotel is Closed/Burned Down" Tactic: Some rogue taxi drivers may tell you your pre-booked hotel is closed or full to take you to a commission-based alternative. Insist on going to your original destination. Always have your hotel's address and contact number handy.

Cultural Immersion: Do's and Don'ts for Travellers in Delhi

Embrace the vibrant culture of North India. A little awareness goes a long way in ensuring a respectful and enriching experience for all travellers in Delhi, especially foreign travellers:

  • Modest Dress: Especially in religious sites and smaller towns, dress conservatively. Cover your shoulders and knees. Loose, breathable clothing is comfortable and respectful. This is particularly important for female travellers in New Delhi.
  • Greetings: A gentle "Namaste" with folded hands is a widely appreciated greeting. Handshakes are common in urban areas, but wait for the other person to initiate.
  • Footwear Etiquette: Always remove your shoes before entering homes, temples, and gurdwaras. Look for designated shoe-keeping areas.
  • Public Displays of Affection (PDA): Keep PDA to a minimum. It's generally frowned upon in public spaces in India.
  • Eating & Hands: Traditionally, eat with your right hand. The left hand is considered unclean.
  • Bargaining: It's expected in local markets for souvenirs and clothes, but do it politely and with a smile. Enjoy the experience!
  • Photography: Always ask permission before photographing people, especially women. Be respectful if someone declines.
  • Religious Sensitivity: Respect local customs in religious places. Avoid pointing your feet towards deities.

Health & Safety Essentials for Your Delhi & North India Trip

Ensuring your well-being is paramount for an enjoyable North India travel experience. Here are key considerations for safety in Delhi for solo travellers and others:

Mosquitoes Risk of Dengue, Malaria (seasonal/regional). Use strong repellent. Wear long sleeves/trousers at dawn/dusk, especially for evening outings in Delhi.
Sun Protection Intense sun, especially in plains of Delhi. Sunscreen, hat, sunglasses. Stay hydrated. Seek shade during peak afternoon hours.
General Safety Petty crime, touts, crowded areas. Keep valuables secure. Avoid isolated areas at night. Trust your instincts. Use official transport. Be particularly vigilant in crowded markets.

Must-See Destinations & Experiences for Travellers in Delhi

From Delhi and New Delhi, a world of wonders awaits. Here are some top destinations and experiences for international travellers in India, including solo travellers:

  1. The Golden Triangle (Delhi - Agra - Jaipur): The classic route, easily accessible from Delhi, covering iconic historical and cultural sites like the Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, and Jaipur's Amber Fort. This is a popular and relatively easy circuit for first-time foreign travellers.
  2. Rishikesh & Haridwar: For spiritual seekers and adventure enthusiasts, these holy towns on the Ganges offer yoga, meditation, and white-water rafting, a few hours' drive from Delhi. Great for solo travellers seeking peace or adventure.
  3. Shimla & Manali (Himachal Pradesh): Head to the Himalayas for stunning mountain landscapes, trekking, and cooler climates, especially during summer. A refreshing escape from the Delhi heat.
  4. Amritsar: Home to the Golden Temple, a serene and spiritually significant site, and the historic Jallianwala Bagh. Easily reachable by train from Delhi. A deeply moving experience for any traveller.
  5. Varanasi: While a bit further, a domestic flight from Delhi can take you to this ancient spiritual city on the Ganges, known for its ghats and evening Ganga Aarti. A profound cultural experience.

Staying Safe & Respectful: Key Tips for Travellers in Delhi & North India

A few mindful practices can greatly enhance your safety and enjoyment, especially for solo and female travellers in Delhi:

  • Maintain Awareness: Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded places like markets and public transport in New Delhi.
  • Secure Valuables: Use money belts or secure inner pockets. Don't flash large amounts of cash or expensive gadgets. Be discreet.
  • Trust Your Gut: If a situation feels uncomfortable or suspicious, politely remove yourself. Your instincts are your best guide, especially for solo female travellers in New Delhi.
  • Respect Local Customs: Your modesty in dress, especially around religious sites, will be appreciated. This shows respect for Indian culture.
  • Be Assertive (Politely): Learn to say a firm but polite 'no thank you' to persistent vendors or touts.
  • Hydrate & Protect: Drink plenty of bottled water and protect yourself from the sun, particularly in Delhi's climate.
  • Research Before You Go: Look up specific customs or advisories for the exact regions you plan to visit. Share your itinerary with a trusted friend or family member.
  • Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency numbers (Police 100, National Emergency 112) and your embassy/consulate handy.

Frequently Asked Questions About Traveling to Delhi & North India

Q: What are the essential documents for foreign travellers arriving at Delhi Airport (IGI)?

A: Foreign travellers need a valid passport, a valid Indian visa (e-Visa printout or stamped visa), and potentially a copy of your flight itinerary and hotel bookings. Ensure your e-Visa is printed and ready for a smooth entry into Delhi.

Q: How do solo female travellers get from New Delhi Airport to their hotel safely?

A: For enhanced safety and convenience for solo female travellers in New Delhi, it's highly recommended to pre-book a taxi through your hotel, use official airport prepaid taxi counters, or use reputable ride-hailing apps like Uber or Ola from designated pick-up points. Always share your ride details with a trusted contact. Avoid unsolicited taxi offers outside the terminal.

Q: What is the best way for travellers to get a local SIM card in Delhi after landing?

A: Travellers in Delhi can purchase a local SIM card (e.g., Airtel, Jio, Vodafone Idea) directly at kiosks within Delhi Airport upon arrival. You'll need your passport, visa, and a passport-sized photo. Activation usually takes a few hours, so plan accordingly.

Q: What cultural customs should foreign travellers be aware of in New Delhi?

A: Modest dressing is advised, especially when visiting religious sites (cover shoulders and knees). Remove shoes before entering temples and homes. Greet with 'Namaste' (folded hands). Avoid public displays of affection. Use your right hand for eating or handing items. These tips are vital for foreign travellers in New Delhi to show respect.

Q: Is it safe to travel solo in Delhi, especially for women?

A: Delhi is generally safe for travellers, but solo female travellers should exercise extra caution. Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas, use trusted transport (like women-only metro coaches or app-based taxis), and be aware of your surroundings. Consider joining group tours for certain excursions. Always trust your instincts, especially when travelling solo in New Delhi.

Q: What are good ways for solo travellers to meet people in New Delhi?

A: Solo travellers in New Delhi can meet people by staying in popular hostels with social common areas, joining walking tours, participating in local workshops (e.g., cooking classes), or attending expat meetups. Co-working cafes are also great spots to connect with locals and other travellers.
