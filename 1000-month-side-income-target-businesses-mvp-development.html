What is MVP Development and Why is it High-Value?

An MVP (Minimum Viable Product) is the version of a new product which allows a team to collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort. In simpler terms, it's the core, essential features of a new app or software, built to launch quickly and test a business idea before committing to a full-scale development.

MVP development is high-value for several reasons:

  • Critical First Step: For startups, an MVP is often their very first tangible product, essential for attracting investors and early adopters.
  • Defined Scope: While complex, MVPs typically have a more defined initial scope compared to an open-ended "full product," making quoting and delivery more straightforward.
  • Gateway to Future Work: Successful MVPs often lead to subsequent development phases, offering potential for recurring referrals.
  • Higher Budgets: Even a "minimum" viable product often involves custom coding and strategic design, leading to project values of $10,000 or more.
